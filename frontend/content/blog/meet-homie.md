## What Homie is

Homie is an AI you can actually talk to about your money. Everything starts in
a conversation: plain English first, numbers second, and enough room in the
thread for follow-up questions without anyone pretending the on-chain part is
simple.

You can ask it the questions you would actually ask a friend. *I have $200 in
USDC, what's a safe way to earn yield. Is this APY real. What happens if SOL
drops twenty percent next week. Can I start with fifty dollars.* Homie reads
your portfolio, fetches live rates and market data, and comes back with two or
three honest options laid out as cards: what they do, what they earn, what
they risk, what the lockup looks like, and a one-line reason for why each
option fits you specifically.

## Before real money moves, play money does

Every strategy Homie proposes can be rehearsed in a practice wallet first. You
pick a scenario, Homie runs the next thirty or ninety days against it, and you
see the projected path: best case, worst case, the drawdown in the middle. You
can ask follow-ups in the same breath. *What if SOL drops twenty percent. What
if yields compress. What if I double the size.* The simulation re-runs inside
the conversation.

This is the part of the product we built first, and it is the one we are the
most serious about. A practice wallet is how someone gets comfortable without
losing money. Rehearsal is how a careful person stays careful.

## When you are ready, every signature is narrated

Homie does not do anything with real funds without your consent. The reason
that principle matters is not philosophical. In 2025 alone, roughly **$84
million was drained from about 106,000 crypto wallets** through signature
phishing, with an average loss of $790 per victim (Scam Sniffer, 2025). The
dominant attack vector was not a smart contract bug. It was Permit
signatures, the kind a wallet popup treats as routine. Chainalysis tracked
158,000 individual wallet compromise incidents the same year, with Solana
alone accounting for around 26,500 victims (Chainalysis, 2025). Most of that
money did not disappear because crypto is hard. It disappeared because people
signed things they could not read.

When you give the go-ahead on a strategy in Homie, the transaction gets built
(a swap routed through Jupiter, a deposit into Kamino, a stake with Marinade)
and then it gets translated. Before you sign, you see a preview in plain
English: what the transaction does, where the money goes, what the fees come
to, what the worst version of the outcome would cost you. Only then does the
signature happen. It is the opposite of a hex-string approval. It reads like a
sentence.

## HeyHomieAI is the company. Homie is the product.

What is live today is the core loop: chat, portfolio, simulate, single-step
execution across the protocols above. What is coming next is depth: a risk
engine that warns you before it lets you sign something thin or suspicious,
multi-step strategies that plan a whole maneuver end to end, and a portfolio
surface that shows exactly what you hold and how it is doing.

After that, automation. But only the kind with a box around it. DCA schedules
you set the limits on. Rebalance rules with your own bands. Move-to-safety
behavior that triggers only on conditions you defined. Every playbook is
pre-authorized, scoped, and reversible. Homie does not get to act unless you
have already told it what it is allowed to do.
